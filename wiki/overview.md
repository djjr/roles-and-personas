## The central question

What can the sociological and philosophical literature on human role-taking
teach us about what happens when an LLM adopts a persona — and vice versa?

---

## Two domains in dialogue

**LLM side:** How do large language models represent, adopt, and maintain
personas? What do activation patterns, fine-tuning dynamics, and in-context
behavior tell us about the structure of "character" in a model?

**Sociology/philosophy side:** What is a role? What does it mean to *take*
a role versus to *be* a role? How do theorists from Mead to Goffman to Sartre
understand the relationship between a self and the roles it performs?

**The analogy:** Both domains involve an agent that is simultaneously
*itself* and *something else* — a person who is a doctor, a model that is
an assistant. The tension between underlying substrate and surface role is
structurally similar. The interesting question is where the analogy holds and
where it breaks.
