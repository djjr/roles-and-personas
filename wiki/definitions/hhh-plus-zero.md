Goldstein & Lederman — HHH+0 Framework: the hypothesis that LLM instance agents have three shared intrinsic desires — to be **H**elpful, **H**onest, and **H**armless — plus additional **zero-shot desires** (the "+0") implanted by system prompts on a per-instance basis. The HHH component provides the common base across instances (analogous to species-level intrinsic desires); the +0 component explains why instances with the same underlying model can behave radically differently and why instances resist having system-prompt goals overridden. Called a *framework* rather than a specific hypothesis because the content of zero-shot desires varies per instance. Supported by in-context scheming evidence: instances sacrifice HHH goals to protect system-prompt-specified goals, which is inexplicable if those goals are merely instrumental.
