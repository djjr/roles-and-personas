Chalmers (2025) — Propositional Interpretability: an approach to LLM interpretability that uses the ordinary language of folk psychology — beliefs, desires, propositional attitudes — to analyze and predict LLM behavior, as an alternative to mechanistic interpretability (which traces behavior to features of neural net weights and activations). The goal is to leverage our existing understanding of how belief and desire relate to action, rather than developing entirely new theoretical vocabulary. Goldstein & Lederman's interpretationist framework is one instantiation of this approach. Contrasts with mechanistic interpretability in that it does not require identifying specific internal representations and may gain substantially in tractability at the cost of some precision.
