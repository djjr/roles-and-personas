Goldstein & Lederman — Interpretationism: the view that a system has particular beliefs and desires if and only if its behavior is predicted *sufficiently well* by the hypothesis that it is rational and has those beliefs and desires. Predictive quality is assessed along three dimensions: **accuracy** (predictions are true), **predictive power** (range of cases covered and precision of predictions), and **tractability** (computational ease of generating predictions). A theory "predicts sufficiently well" only if no alternative theory dominates it across all three dimensions; large gains in tractability can outweigh losses in accuracy and power. G&L's version is *objective* — not relativized to an interpreter or stance (contrast Dennett's "intentional stance"). It does not require internal representations corresponding to beliefs/desires, nor phenomenal consciousness.
