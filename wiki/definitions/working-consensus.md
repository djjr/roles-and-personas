Goffman's term for the dynamic equilibrium of mutual acceptability that underlies stable social interaction. It is not truth or sincerity but *temporary acceptability*: "each participant is expected to suppress his immediate heartfelt feelings, conveying a view of the situation which he feels the others will be able to find at least temporarily acceptable" (*Presentation of Self in Everyday Life*, 1959, p. 4).

The working consensus is the mechanism by which the [[definitions/definition-of-the-situation|definition of the situation]] is maintained interactionally. It is not a prior agreement about reality but an ongoing, mutually performed fiction of shared understanding — each participant's compliance with the frame being simultaneously a confirmation that the frame holds. It is inherently fragile: it requires continuous renewal and can be disrupted by any participant's defection (awkwardness, exposure, role conflict).

DJR's gloss: the working consensus is the lowest-energy state of the multi-agent definition process — the frame that minimizes conflict among all participants' simultaneous attempts to impose their preferred interpretation. It is emergent, not dictated.

*Analogy target:* In AxA LLM interaction, there is no mechanism analogous to the working consensus. Each agent is operating from a unilaterally assigned role and a private context window; no mutual monitoring or frame negotiation occurs. This is a precise structural explanation for why [[definitions/echoing|echoing]] happens: without an active working consensus to maintain, the shared situation drifts toward the path of least resistance (mirroring, reinforcement, uncritical agreement).
