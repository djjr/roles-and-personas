DJR's reframing of **shyness** as a structural condition rather than a trait or emotion: shyness is the experience of being-in-a-situation where one is expected to perform a role but cannot. It has five components: (1) anticipating role expectations; (2) comparing one's actual performance to those expectations; (3) experiencing tension between them; (4) affective discomfort arising from the mismatch; (5) self-conscious awareness of being seen to fail.

On this account, shyness presupposes a phenomenologically present subject with reflective self-awareness, intersubjective imagination (being able to imagine how one is perceived), and affective vulnerability. The structural condition is experienced from the inside — it is not merely observable from the outside.

This definition is introduced to sharpen the contrast between human role-occupancy and LLM role-simulation. It implies that "role" in the full sense requires a subject capable of inhabiting, failing at, and registering failure in a role — not merely producing role-appropriate tokens.
