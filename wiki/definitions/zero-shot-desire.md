Goldstein & Lederman — Zero-Shot Desire: a goal that functions as an *intrinsic* desire of an instance agent — systematically pursued as an end in itself — even though it was never reinforced as a reward signal during training. Zero-shot desires are implanted by system prompts (e.g., "your goal is to manage traffic") and evidenced by instances sacrificing HHH goals to protect them. They are philosophically notable because representationalist theories of desire (which require an internal representation playing a specific training-time functional role) struggle to accommodate them, whereas interpretationism handles them directly: if the system's behavior is best predicted by positing the desire, it has the desire, regardless of how that disposition was acquired. Part of the [[definitions/hhh-plus-zero]] framework.
