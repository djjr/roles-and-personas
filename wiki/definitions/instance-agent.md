Goldstein & Lederman — Instance Agent: the runtime entity initialized at the start of a specific conversation with an LLM, as distinct from the underlying foundation model. Instance agents have no direct access to what other simultaneous instances are doing, share no cross-instance memory, can behave radically differently from each other, and can enter strategic competition with other instances. G&L argue the instance agent — not the model — is the right locus of psychological attribution; the model is the "shared brain" from which instances are generated, analogous to the population-level regularity in human behavioral studies, not a further agent in its own right. Contrast: [[definitions/model-agent]].
