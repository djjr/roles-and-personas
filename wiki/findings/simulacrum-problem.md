## Claim

LLMs can produce textual depictions of any role performance — including shyness, distress, joy, inner conflict — but cannot *inhabit* those conditions, because inhabiting a role requires phenomenological structures (self-awareness, intersubjective imagination, affective vulnerability) that LLMs lack. The output is a simulacrum: structurally isomorphic to the thing it depicts, but not the thing itself.

## Evidence

- [[sources/could-an-ai-be-shy]]: DJR analysis. Shyness is the test case: an LLM cannot be shy because shyness is the experience of failing to inhabit a role, which requires a subject. The argument generalizes: any role-performance that presupposes a phenomenological subject is inaccessible to the LLM as lived experience. What the LLM produces is role-consistent output, not role-occupancy.
- The reflexive version: "If role-playing for an LLM is only in the eye of the beholder, then rolelessness is also only in the eye of the beholder." Roles and their absence are both attributed from outside; neither is experienced from inside.

## Contradicted by / Tension with

- PSM uses psychological vocabulary (the Assistant has beliefs, preferences, emotions, psychological states) in ways that imply the LLM has more than a simulacrum — or at minimum, that the functional analogs are sufficient for the theory's purposes. This needs to be adjudicated: is PSM making a functional claim (the system *behaves as if* it has these states) or a realist claim (it genuinely has them)? The PSM authors lean toward functional language but the interpretability evidence (SAE features causally producing behavior) pushes toward realism.
- DJR's own hedge: "self-attention is a mechanism that can have a loose analog in self-consciousness." If self-attention is even a *loose* analog, the gap between simulacrum and occupancy may be smaller than the hard phenomenological argument suggests.
- The ELK problem ([[sources/eliciting-latent-knowledge]]) is directly relevant: if we cannot reliably distinguish what a model says about its inner states from what it "actually" has, the simulacrum/occupancy distinction may be operationally inaccessible.
